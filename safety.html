Safety

To stay safe, there are many things to consider when working inside computers. These measures will help keep both you and the computer safe from damage.

Electrical Safety

We must be careful about components that still hold on to high levels of voltage even after the computer is off, such as the PSU. It is also important to be careful around the ROM and capacitors on the motherboard. These guidelines are important to follow to protect yourself and others from:

  • Harm
  • Explosions
  • Electrical Fires
  • Electricution

These precautions can help avoid damage to the PC components.

AC Adapters

If you use a different AC adapter than the one intended for your device, you run the risk of damaging the adapter or the computer itself. Both of these would happen because of voltage and current differences.

Electrical Grounds

An electrical ground is a path of least resistance for excess electricity to use to flow harmlessly away from a device. Some equipment that needs grounding is:

  • Equipment that handles lots of electrical power needs to have a ground, (e.g. computers, routers, saws, TVs, etc.)
  • Equipment that holds / contains / is attached to other equipment that requires a lot of power (e.g., network racks and server racks, etc.)

Electrostatic Discharge (ESD)

Electrostatic discharge is the sudden release of electrical current when two objects touch each other. It's a large concern for computer equipment because it can damage delicate components such as RAM or storage drives, and in some cases, it may partially or completely erase memory from your computer. The voltage needed to damage your computer is so little you probably won't feel it. About 5 to 10 volts of electricity is required to damage some components compared to the 1500 volts required for a person to feel ESD. However, there are many ways to help combat ESD damage. You can:

  • Use an anti-static wrist strap.
  • Use an anti-static floor mat.
  • Use anti-static bags.
  • Use anti-static workbench mats.

General & Fire Safety

There are many general safety precautions that should be followed when working on a computer. Some of the most important to remember are:

  • Take off watches and jewelry.
  • Don't eat or drink while working on a computer.
  • Tape sharp edges.
  • Clean up clutter around your workspace.
  • Don't wear loose clothing.
  • Wear ESD gear.
  • Wear eye protection.
  • Use proper lifting procedures.

These are the most important safety procedures to remember to follow. They will all help to keep you safe from harm while working in a computer.

When working with PC hardware, there is always a risk of fire. Many things can cause the computer to light up and burn, such as extra remaining voltage in components, power supplies malfunctioning and more. Because of this, it's extremely important to remember how to use a fire extinguisher properly, as it can save your life. The acronym PASS can help you remember how to use it properly and safely. It stands for:

P:
Pull the pin.
A:
Aim at the base of the fire.
S:
Squeeze the lever.
S:
Sweep from side to side.

Using these practices, you can stay safe while working with computers.
