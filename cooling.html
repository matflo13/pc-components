Cooling Systems

Form Factor Type Description
Case Fans Active Takes in cold air and spreads it around the computer, bringing heat away from your components. Usually, there are at least two fans; one to take air in, and one to push air out. They are active cooling systems because they need power to function.
CPU Heat Sinks Passive A heat sink works by absorbing heat into each of its metal fins, and dissipates it into the air around the CPU. This helps to conduct heat away from the PC. Between the CPU and the heat sink, we place thermal paste to fill in microscopic air gaps. It's passive because it can function without power.
